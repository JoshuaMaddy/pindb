# Editor Guide

Welcome to PinDB's editing system. This guide explains how contributions work, what happens after you submit something, and what is expected of editors.

---

## The Editor Role

Editors are trusted contributors who can add and update content in PinDB — pins, artists, shops, tags, and pin sets. The editor role is granted by an administrator. If you believe you should have editor access, apply through the community Discord.

Administrators are editors too, with one difference: their submissions are approved automatically.

---

## What Editors Can Do

As an editor, you can:

- **Create** new pins, artists, shops, tags, and pin sets
- **Edit** any existing approved entry — not just your own

All submissions enter a pending state and are reviewed by an administrator before becoming visible to the public.

---

## The Pending System

### New entries

When you create a new pin, artist, shop, tag, or pin set, it is marked as **pending**. It will not appear in search results or public listings until an administrator approves it.

While pending, the entry is visible to you and to administrators. Other regular users and guests cannot see it. In selection lists (for example, when associating an artist with a pin), pending entries appear with a **(P)** prefix so you can still reference them while they await approval.

**Pin approval cascades.** When an administrator approves a pin, any pending artists, shops, or tags attached to that pin are also approved at the same time. You do not need to wait for each dependency to be approved separately before submitting a pin.

### Edits to existing entries

When you edit an already-approved entry, your changes are held as a **pending edit**. The original approved content remains visible to everyone until the edit is reviewed. Administrators view your proposed changes and can approve or reject them.

---

## Approval and Rejection

Administrators review all pending submissions and edits via the approval queue.

- **Approved** — your submission goes live immediately.
- **Rejected** — the submission is declined. A rejected entry is no longer visible to users. If you believe the rejection was in error you are welcome to create a new submission from scratch or reach out to an administrator for context on why something was declined.

---

## What You Can Edit

Editors can propose edits to any approved entry, regardless of who created it.

However, edits to your own pending entries (not yet approved) work differently: you can edit a pending entry you created, but only while it is still pending. Once approved, any further changes go through the normal pending edit flow.

---

## Quality Expectations

The approval queue is a shared resource. Submissions that are incomplete, duplicated, or poorly researched slow down the process for everyone. Before submitting, please:

- **Check for duplicates.** Search before creating. Artists, shops, and tags are shared across many pins — adding a duplicate creates confusion that is difficult to clean up.
- **Use accurate names.** Prefer the name the artist or shop uses publicly. Avoid nicknames or abbreviations unless they are the primarily known name.
- **Associate carefully.** When associating artists, shops, or tags with a pin, verify the association is correct. Incorrect links are harder to spot than missing ones.

Quality contributions build a database that the community can rely on. Thank you for taking the time to get it right!
