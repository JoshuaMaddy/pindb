# Artist Creation Guide

Artists are the creators — illustrators, designers, and studios — credited for a pin's artwork. An artist entry is shared across all pins they created, so a well-maintained artist page benefits every one of those pins.

---

## Before You Create an Artist

**Search first.** Artists must be unique by name. Check for alternate capitalizations or abbreviated forms before creating a new entry. If the artist already exists under a different name, add the new name as an alias.

## Artist vs. Shop

An artist is a creator; a shop is a seller. Often they are the same person — a solo creator who runs their own shop. In that case, create **both** an artist entry and a shop entry and link the relevant pins to each.

If a studio employs multiple artists but operates as a single brand, individual artist credits per pin are preferred if possible. If artists are not specified per pin, omit them entirely. Note recurring or foundational artists in the shop's description!

---

## Artist Name

Use the name the artist publicly uses for their pin work. This is often a handle or studio name rather than a legal name — use whatever appears on their storefront, social media, or the pin packaging itself.

If the artist goes by multiple names, enter the most widely recognized one as the canonical name and add the others as aliases.

## Description (optional)

A short, factual biography or description. Good things to include: the type of art or pins they specialize in, notable series or collaborations, and the region they operate from. Keep it neutral and factual. You may include the artist's official blurb if they have one.

## Active

Mark an artist as **inactive** if they have permanently stopped creating pins. Do not mark them inactive because they have been quiet for a while — creative schedules vary.

## Aliases

Alternate names or handles the artist can be found by:
- A legal name if they are also publicly known by it
- A former handle or name used before a rebrand

## Links

External URLs for the artist — their personal website, Etsy, Instagram, Ko-fi, etc. These help users support the artist directly.
